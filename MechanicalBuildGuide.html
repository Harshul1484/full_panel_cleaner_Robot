HOW THE ROBOT WORKS — READ THIS FIRST
Simple explanation of every mechanical part before you build anything
🚗 How it moves

Two GF555 motors — one per side. Left motor drives the left track, right motor drives the right track independently. By running them at different speeds or opposite directions the robot steers and spins in place — called differential drive, exactly like a tank.

🧹 How it cleans

Two brush rollers (front and rear, each 650mm wide) spin and scrub the panel. A water jet pipe sprays water ahead of the front brush. Together they wet-scrub the whole panel surface.

🔄 How it knows where it is

IR sensors at each corner look down. When a sensor sees no surface (panel edge), the robot knows to turn. It sweeps in an S-pattern row by row until the full panel is clean.

BUILDING THE CHASSIS FRAME — STEP BY STEP
Build the skeleton first. Everything else bolts to this.
STEP 2.1
Prepare the Side Plates

Take both laser-cut mild steel side plates (820×150×3mm). Before drilling, do this:

  • File all edges with a flat file — remove burrs from laser cutting
  • Mark center line along the length at 75mm height from bottom edge (half of 150mm)
  • This center line is where all shaft holes go
  • Mark hole positions: bearing holes at X=30mm and X=790mm from front
  • Mark sprocket shaft holes: spaced every ~45mm along the center line between the two bearing holes
  • Mark 3 cross-brace bolt holes: at X=60mm, X=410mm, X=760mm — at both 20mm from top AND 20mm from bottom (total 6 holes per plate)
STEP 2.2
Drill All Holes in Side Plates DRILL
  • Use a center punch first to mark every hole — stops the drill from wandering
  • Bearing holes: Start with Ø5mm pilot drill, then open up to final bearing OD size. Use step drill bit or hole saw.
  • Sprocket shaft holes: Drill Ø10.5mm (slightly larger than 10mm shaft for easy fit)
  • Cross-brace holes: Drill Ø4.5mm for M4 bolts
  • Motor mount holes: Drill 4× Ø4.5mm in pattern matching GF555 motor face
  • After drilling, use a deburring tool or countersink bit to clean each hole opening
  • Do BOTH plates together — clamp them face-to-face and drill through both at once so holes are identical
✓ Drill through both side plates clamped together — guarantees perfect alignment. Use a scrap wood board underneath to get clean exit holes.
STEP 2.3
Assemble the Frame Box
  • Lay the left side plate flat on workbench
  • Stand the front end plate (650×150mm) perpendicular at the front end — clamp with C-clamps
  • Stand the rear end plate at the rear end — clamp
  • Insert the 3× cross braces between the side plates at their marked positions
  • Check all is square with a try square — adjust before bolting
  • Bolt cross braces to left plate: M4×12mm bolts + flat washer + nyloc nut — do not fully tighten yet
  • Now attach right side plate — bolt through to cross braces
  • Check squareness again — measure diagonals (should be equal)
  • Once square, fully tighten all M4 bolts. Torque to hand-tight + ¼ turn
  • Bolt front and rear end plates using M4×20mm + nyloc nuts
STEP 2.4
Install Bearings in Side Plates
  • Clean bearing holes with a rag — no dust or chips inside
  • Apply thin layer of bearing retainer (Loctite 641) around the bearing OD
  • Press bearing into hole using a mallet and a block of wood — tap evenly around the race, never hit the inner ring
  • Check bearing sits flush with plate surface — should not stick out
  • Let Loctite cure for 10 minutes before loading
  • Repeat for all bearing positions (2 main + 6 idler shaft bearing holes)
⚠ Never hammer directly on a bearing. Always use a block of wood or a socket the same OD as the bearing outer race. Hitting the bearing directly damages the balls inside.
STEP 2.5
Mount Electronics Tray Inside Chassis
  • Take the laser-cut electronics tray (600×200×2mm mild steel — paint top surface before fitting PCBs)
  • Drill 4× M3 mounting holes in the tray corners
  • Insert M3×10mm bolts from outside the side plate through to the tray
  • Use M3 nyloc nuts inside — position tray 50mm up from bottom of chassis
  • Tray should sit level and horizontal inside the chassis

DRIVE SYSTEM — 2× GF555 MOTORS + SPROCKETS + ST-320 TRACK (DIFFERENTIAL DRIVE)
This is what makes the robot move on the solar panel
ℹ Differential drive: This robot has TWO GF555 motors — one driving the left track, one driving the right track independently. Each motor has its own BTS7960 H-bridge. When both run forward at the same speed the robot goes straight. When one runs faster it turns toward the slower side. When they run opposite directions the robot spins in place.
STEP 3.1
Mount GF555 LEFT Motor (drives left track)
  • Bolt the left motor mount plate (140×140mm mild steel) to the inside of the LEFT side of the chassis using M4×12mm bolts — or weld it directly to the side plate for maximum strength
  • Position: centered at ~357mm from the front of the chassis (midpoint of the 715mm side plate)
  • Align GF555 motor face to the mount plate — 4 holes pattern
  • Bolt GF555 using M4×20mm bolts + flat washer + spring washer — apply Loctite 243 on threads
  • GF555 output shaft must point toward the left side plate bearing — shaft runs left-to-right through the bearing and out
  • Check alignment: shaft must be centered in the bearing. No side load. Tighten bolts while shaft is spinning freely.
STEP 3.1b
Mount GF555 RIGHT Motor (drives right track)
  • Identical procedure to Step 3.1 — mirror image on the RIGHT side of the chassis
  • Both motors are at the same lengthwise position (midpoint ≈410mm) — they are side-by-side on opposite sides
  • The two motor shafts are on opposite sides — LEFT motor shaft exits left plate, RIGHT motor shaft exits right plate
  • Label motors with tape: "L" and "R" to avoid wiring confusion later
STEP 3.2
Attach the Shaft Couplings (2× copling)
  • Slide one 3D-printed coupling (copling, 41×30×30mm) onto each GF555 output shaft
  • Each coupling connects: GF555 shaft → drive sprocket shaft for that side
  • Tighten M3 set screws in each coupling using a hex key — apply Loctite 243 on set screw threads
  • Pull each coupling by hand — must not slip on the shaft at all
  • You should have 2 couplings total — one per GF555 motor
STEP 3.3
Install the 17 Sprocket Shafts (shaft_sheni)
  • Take each 75mm long Ø10mm steel rod (you cut 17 of these earlier)
  • Slide through the Ø10.5mm holes in the left side plate
  • Slip a bearing onto the shaft from the inside
  • Slide through the same hole position on the right side plate
  • Put an E-clip (circlip) in the groove at each shaft end — this stops the shaft sliding out
  • Check shaft spins freely in the bearing — no stiffness
  • Repeat for all 27 shafts per side (54 total)
ℹ The 54 sprocket shafts (27 per side) span the full 715mm length of each side plate — they are what the track rides on. Space them evenly ~25mm apart along the length.
STEP 3.4
Press Sprockets onto Shafts (sheni)
  • Slide one sprocket onto each of the 27 shafts per side (54 total)
  • Position sprocket in the CENTER of the shaft (equal gap on both sides)
  • Tighten the M3 set screw in the sprocket hub against the shaft — use Loctite 243
  • Spin each sprocket by hand — should spin freely with the shaft, no wobble
  • The 6 double-strand idler sprockets (6260K9, 72×72×43mm) — 3 per side — go at the tensioner positions front, center, and rear of each track. These are larger than the regular sprockets and keep track tension correct.
STEP 3.5
Install the ST-320 Rubber Track
  • This is the rubber chain/track that wraps around all sprockets like a tank
  • Start at the DRIVE sprocket (connected to GF555 motor)
  • Loop the track around the front sprocket, then along the top row of sprockets, around the rear, and back along the bottom
  • Thread track carefully over all 27 sprockets per side — do not force it
  • Connect track ends: most ST-320 tracks have a master link — push the pin through and clip the E-clip
  • Check track tension: press the middle of the bottom span — should deflect about 5–8mm. Not too tight, not too loose.
✓ Use the loleh (tensioner pulley, 445×20×75mm) to adjust tension — loosen the tensioner bolt, push the tensioner outward until correct tension, then re-tighten. The loleh_dakheli (inner guide, 715×149×20mm) keeps the track centered.
⚠ Track too tight = motor stalls and overheats. Track too loose = track jumps off sprockets. 5–8mm deflection at center is correct.
STEP 3.6
Test Both Drive Motors Before Continuing
  • Connect LEFT GF555 motor temporarily to a 24V power supply — left track should move forward
  • Disconnect. Connect RIGHT GF555 motor to 24V — right track should move forward
  • Test both together at 24V — robot should drive straight without pulling to one side
  • Listen for: grinding (bad alignment), clicking (track too loose), squealing (bearing dry)
  • Test differential spin: apply 24V to LEFT forward + RIGHT reversed — robot should spin in place clockwise
  • All good → disconnect power and continue to Phase 4
  • If one track is faster: slightly reduce that motor's run time during the test to compare torque

BRUSH + WASH ASSEMBLY (× 2 identical) BUY 3D PRINT
Build this assembly twice — one for front, one for rear of the robot
ℹ This is the wash-assemble1 from your model. You have 2 identical assemblies. Build one, test it, then build the second identical to the first.
STEP 4.1
Build the Brush Roller (brash — 650×70×70mm)
  • Take the Ø20mm × 670mm mild steel round bar (brush roller shaft) — sand smooth, paint with primer before fitting
  • Slide bearing (5972K244, 30×30×7mm) onto each end of the shaft
  • Push each bearing into the brush assembly side bracket hole
  • Wrap microfiber roller sleeve over the mild steel shaft — the sleeve is the actual brush material that contacts the panel
  • Secure sleeve with stainless zip ties at both ends and middle (3 zip ties per roller)
  • Check roller spins freely in the side brackets
STEP 4.2
Mount 6331K61 DC Motor for Brush Drive
  • Print DC-motor-bracket (61×36×35mm) in PETG
  • Bolt the 6331K61 motor (30×37×83mm) into the printed bracket using M3×10mm screws
  • Mount the bracket onto the brush assembly side plate using 3273T51 loop hangers — M3×16mm bolts through loop hanger holes
  • Motor shaft must be parallel to the brush roller shaft — check with a ruler
  • Motor shaft end should be at the same height as the brush shaft end (both shafts coplanar)
STEP 4.3
Install Timing Belt Pulleys + Belt
  • Slide the small timing belt pulley (1277N723, 54mm dia) onto the motor shaft
  • Tighten set screw with M3 hex key — Loctite 243 on thread
  • Slide the large timing belt pulley (1277N725, 57mm dia) onto the brush roller shaft end
  • Tighten set screw
  • Loop the timing belt (Belt1-3, 107mm span) over both pulleys
  • Adjust motor bracket position by loosening loop hangers — move motor away from roller until belt is taut (should not slip when you press it)
  • Lock motor bracket position — tighten all loop hanger bolts
  • Use the 4147N173 flexible shaft coupling if the motor shaft and brush shaft are slightly misaligned (connects motor shaft extension to brush shaft)
STEP 4.4
Install Water Jet Pipe (water-jet — 500mm)
  • Take the 500mm mild steel tube (or 304 stainless tube if available) with 1.5mm spray holes drilled every 50mm along the bottom — stainless is better here since water flows inside
  • Attach barbed tube fittings (9675T18) at both ends of the tube using worm-drive hose clamps (5011T11)
  • Mount the water jet tube on the brush assembly frame — above and slightly in front of the brush roller
  • Use M3×10mm bolts through small drilled holes in the tube wall to fix it to the side brackets
  • Connect silicone hose from T-part-assemble to the barbed fitting
STEP 4.5
Assemble T-Part Water Splitters (×4 total — 2 per brush assembly)
  • Each brush assembly (wash-assemble1) has 2× T-part-assemble — so you need 4 T-parts total for the whole robot
  • Each T-part-assemble = T-cleaner-body + head-of-T-cleaner joined together (580×42×23mm full length)
  • The two T-parts in each brush assembly split water to the left and right halves of the brush roller
  • Print each in 3 sections (each ~193mm long) in PETG at 100% wall thickness for water tightness
  • Join sections with M3×16mm bolts and silicone sealant on the joint faces — let sealant cure 24h before water testing
  • Mount both T-parts along the brush assembly top rail using M3 bolts through the T-part base
  • Water path: pump → main hose → FRONT brush T-parts (×2) → front water-jets; pump → branch hose → REAR brush T-parts (×2) → rear water-jets
ℹ Total water system: 1 pump → solenoid valve → splits to front + rear hoses → each goes to 2× T-parts on that brush assembly → 4 water-jet spray lines total across the robot width.
STEP 4.6
Mount Brush Assembly to Chassis
  • Front brush assembly: slide into front slot of chassis, between the track on left and right
  • Use connector and connector2 (3D printed) to lock the assembly to the chassis sides
  • Bolt connectors with M4×30mm bolts through chassis side plate → connector → brush frame
  • Use hinge pin (21×4×19mm printed housing + M4 bolt as pin) to allow slight float — brush can tilt ±2° to follow panel surface
  • Attach splash guard (gard, 615×120mm mild steel 2mm, bent to shape) over the brush roller — curves downward on each side. Paint both faces before fitting.
  • Bolt splash guard with M3×10mm through the side brackets
  • Repeat for rear brush assembly
✓ The slight float via hinge pins is important — solar panels are not perfectly flat. The brush needs to self-adjust to maintain contact across the full 650mm width.

IMPORTANT
IR Sensor Corner Bracket — How to Make It
  • Print the IR sensor L-bracket in PETG (40×40×20mm)
  • One face of the L attaches to the corner of the chassis (M3 bolt into chassis)
  • The other face of the L is horizontal — sensor mounts on it pointing DOWN
  • The sensor face should be 8–12mm above the panel when the robot sits on the panel
  • Angle the sensor slightly outward (10°) — this detects the edge 1–2cm before the track reaches it, giving the robot time to stop
  • After mounting, adjust the small potentiometer on the sensor with a screwdriver until LED on sensor goes ON over the panel and OFF over air/edge

MECHANICAL CHECKLIST BEFORE FIRST RUN
Go through every item — do not power on until all boxes are checked

Frame & Drive

  • All M4 frame bolts tightened (no hand-loose bolts)
  • Nyloc nuts used on all vibrating joints (track area)
  • Loctite applied to all set screws (motor coupling, sprockets)
  • All bearings pressed fully flush — none sticking out
  • All 54 sprocket shafts (27 per side) have E-clips on both ends
  • ST-320 track master link clip is fully seated
  • Track tension: 5–8mm deflection at center span
  • Track centered on sprockets — not riding to one side
  • BOTH motor couplings tight — pull by hand, should not slip
  • LEFT motor coupling and RIGHT motor coupling both checked separately
  • Rotate each drive shaft by hand — that track moves smoothly, no stiff spots

Brush & Water

  • Both brush rollers spin freely by hand
  • Timing belts are taut — does not skip teeth when pressed
  • Belt pulleys set-screwed and Loctited on shafts
  • Splash guards bolted on — no loose edges
  • All hose connections have clamps fully tightened
  • Fill tank with water — leave 5 min — no drips anywhere
  • Water jet spray holes all open (poke with pin if blocked)
  • T-part joints sealed with silicone — no leaks
  • Brush microfiber sleeves secured with zip ties
  • Hinge pins inserted — brush can float ±2° but not fall off

Sensors

  • All 4 IR sensors pointing straight down at corners
  • IR sensor height: 8–12mm above a flat surface when robot sits on it
  • Pot on each IR sensor adjusted — LED ON over surface, OFF over air
  • Both ultrasonic sensors pointing forward, horizontally
  • IMU mounted flat — verify with spirit level
  • All sensor wires tied — cannot catch on moving parts
  • Dock IR receiver positioned at rear center

General Safety

  • No loose wires near track or brush rollers
  • All cable ties trimmed — no sharp protruding ends
  • Electronics tray bolted — does not rattle when chassis shaken
  • Battery strapped with velcro — does not slide
  • Top cover sits flush — no pinched wires
  • Robot placed on flat surface — sits stable without rocking
